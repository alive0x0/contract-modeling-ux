Problem · 2:00–5:00

The Problem We're Solving

What breaks today without intelligent prioritization

⏱ 3 minutes
🎙 Say This

"Data miners are responsible for clearing a large universe of TINs — but the system today gives them no direction. The result is wasted effort, missed deadlines, and recovery dollars left on the table."

"There are three specific gaps I want to highlight, because the design directly addresses each one."

01
No Smart Ordering
TINs aren't sorted by COB spend. A $50K opportunity looks identical to a $500 one. Data miners may work low-value items first.
02
No Real Due Dates
Due dates are either missing, manually guessed, or stuck on sentinel values like 12/31/9999. No tie to contract negotiation deadlines.
03
No Manager Override
Market leads have no way to set or override priority inside the tool. Guidance happens via email, chat, or word of mouth.
💡 Director Framing

"Every one of these gaps is a leakage point — where recoverable COB dollars either don't get worked, get worked too late, or get worked in the wrong order. The design closes all three."

Zone 2A · 9:00–12:00

Smart TIN Table — Intelligent Sorting

System-generated order so data miners always start with what matters most

⏱ 3 minutes
🎙 Say This

"This is the core of the Claim Universe — the TIN table. In the current state, TINs appear in no meaningful order. What we're building is an intelligent sort that runs automatically, using three rules stacked in priority order."

1
Highest COB Spend → Lowest
TINs are sorted by total COB spend in descending order. The biggest recovery opportunities always rise to the top. A data miner who opens their queue at 8am sees the most valuable work first — without any guesswork.
2
Existing COB Project TINs Bump Up
If a TIN is already associated with an established COB project, it gets elevated within its spend tier. This respects the work already invested in project setup and ensures continuity — data miners don't abandon active projects to chase new TINs.
3
Market Lead Priority Override (1 / 2 / 3)
The system generates a default priority. But the market lead has full manual override — they can set any TIN to Priority 1, 2, or 3 directly in the table. This control stays with the human, not the algorithm.
Zone 2B · 12:00–15:00

Smart TIN Table — Due Date Logic

Connecting claims work to real contract deadlines — automatically

⏱ 3 minutes
🎙 Say This

"One of the most persistent pain points in the current tool is due dates. Today, many TINs have no due date, a manually guessed one, or worse — a placeholder value of 12/31/9999 that means nothing. A data miner with no due date has no urgency signal."

"We're replacing that with calculated due dates anchored to real contract deadlines."

Source Logic

Facility TINs PMT → Next Negotiation Date
Professional TINs Contract Modeling → Next Production Date
Calculation Apply formula → optimal due date

Edge Cases — Manual Override Required

⚠ No date found in PMT or Contract Modeling

⚠ Sentinel value: 12/31/9999 detected

In either case, the due date field is left blank and surfaced in the "Awaiting Action" card at the top. The market lead sets it manually — and until they do, the TIN does not appear on data miner screens.

💡 Director Framing

"The due date is not just a label — it's the activation gate. If a TIN has no due date, it's invisible to data miners. This gives the market lead meaningful control: when they set a due date, they're deliberately putting that work into the queue. Nothing slips in accidentally."
